Tashi Delek

About the Film

Tashi Delek — a Dzongkha greeting meaning "good luck" or "auspicious blessings" — is a short documentary that asks a question most countries don't: what if national success was measured by happiness rather than GDP?

Bhutan is the only country in the world to officially measure Gross National Happiness (GNH) — a policy framework that weights psychological wellbeing, cultural preservation, environmental sustainability, and good governance alongside economic development. The film travels through Bhutan to explore what this means in practice: how do ordinary Bhutanese people experience happiness, and what can the rest of the world learn from their approach?

The Story Behind It

I traveled to Bhutan with a small crew and spent time in villages, monasteries, and schools across the country — talking to farmers, monks, students, and government officials. The film is structured around three questions: What is happiness? How do you measure it? And what do you sacrifice to protect it?

Bhutan's model is not without tension. The country has grappled with the influence of television, internet, and outside culture since they arrived in the 1990s. The film explores that friction honestly — this is not a romanticized portrait, but a curious one.

Recognition

Tashi Delek was selected and screened at the Indie Meme Film Festival — a South Asian arts festival celebrating independent cinema.
